Food · Energy · Water Nexus

Food, energy, and water are one system.

I develop human-centered AI for the food, energy, and water nexus, bringing machine learning and neural networks to coupled sustainability problems so a decision in one system accounts for its cost in the other two. My work optimises how scarce resources are shared, and keeps human expertise inside the loop.

Senior Lecturer at the University of Lagos and, in 2026, an Eric and Wendy Schmidt AI in Science Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Chicago, working with Prof. Junhong Chen across UChicago and Argonne National Laboratory.

Research focus

The nexus trade-off space.

Food

Food production and security

Optimization and IoT for sustainable food production, from hydroponic monitoring and control to scheduling renewable energy for agriculture.

Water

Water monitoring and quality

Hybrid machine learning and embedded IoT systems that watch water quality, then fold that data into the wider resource picture.

Energy

Energy and renewables

Sizing and scheduling autonomous renewable energy systems, and recovering solar performance, so energy choices respect food and water limits.

The nexus is the point. Optimising one system on its own usually moves the burden onto another. My models treat the three as a coupled whole, so the trade-offs are on the table before the decision is made.

Method

From sensors to decisions, and back.

A useful model closes the loop. Sensors feed a coupled meta-model, optimization proposes a plan, human expertise reviews it, and what happens in the field updates the next run.
