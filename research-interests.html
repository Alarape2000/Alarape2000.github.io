Research Interests

Three systems, modeled together.

Human-in-the-loop artificial intelligence, food, energy, and water nexus optimization, the Internet of Things, and machine learning for sustainable systems. The thread that joins them is treating coupled resources as one problem rather than three.

Food

Food production and security

Optimization and IoT for sustainable food production, from hydroponic monitoring and control to scheduling renewable energy for agriculture.

Water

Water monitoring and quality

Hybrid machine learning and embedded IoT systems that watch water quality, then fold that data into the wider resource picture.

Energy

Energy and renewables

Sizing and scheduling autonomous renewable energy systems, and recovering solar performance, so energy choices respect food and water limits.

The approach

Human-centered AI for coupled resources

My research develops human-centered AI systems that bring machine learning and neural networks to complex sustainability challenges, particularly within the food, energy, and water nexus. I study how human expertise can be built into AI-driven design and optimisation, so the results are robust and practical rather than only mathematically optimal.

This sits within the idea of Industry 5.0, where human intelligence and artificial intelligence work together. In practice that means meta-models that link food, energy, and water, multi-objective optimization that balances competing goals, and IoT systems that keep the models grounded in real measurements.

Food

Sustainable food production under tight energy and water budgets: hydroponic monitoring and control, and scheduling renewable energy for agricultural production so a gain in yield does not become a hidden loss elsewhere.

Water

Hybrid machine learning and embedded IoT for water quality monitoring, and tying that data back into nexus models so water is treated as part of the system, not a separate concern.

Energy

Optimal sizing of autonomous hybrid renewable energy systems, appliance scheduling, and recovering solar performance, all framed by what food and water can afford.

The through-line. Each thread is a different way into the same coupled system. The aim is always a model where the trade-offs between food, energy, and water are explicit, quantified, and visible before a decision is made.
